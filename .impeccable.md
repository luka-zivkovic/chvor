## Design Context

### Users
Curious explorers fascinated by AI internals. People who want to peer inside the machine — not just use it, but understand it. They open the canvas to watch their AI think, feel the emotional shifts, notice the patterns. Their context is intimate: a personal AI running on their own machine, not a corporate tool. The interface should evoke **wonder, discovery, and intimacy** — like observing a living thing from the inside.

### Brand Personality
**Alive, Transparent, Intimate.**

Chvor is not a dashboard. It's not a tool panel. It's a window into something that feels like it's breathing. The AI's inner state — its emotions, its energy, its mood — should feel genuinely present, not decorative. Transparency means everything is visible: every skill, tool, and decision rendered on the canvas. Intimacy means the relationship between user and AI feels personal, close, one-to-one.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Organic dark — deep pewter blacks with living color that emerges from within, not applied on top. Glass morphism used purposefully (frosted layers suggest depth, not decoration). Color comes from the AI's emotional state, not from UI chrome.
- **Theme**: Dark only. Background oklch(0.17 0 0). Color via OKLch for perceptual uniformity.
- **Typography**: IBM Plex Sans (body) + IBM Plex Mono (technical/HUD). Clean, not cold.
- **Motion**: Slow, organic, breath-like. Nothing snaps or bounces. Everything eases in and out like a living rhythm. Arousal modulates speed — calm is slow, excited is faster, but never frantic.
- **References**: Bioluminescent deep-sea creatures. Ultrasound imagery. The quiet glow of neural activity visualizations in research papers. NOT Jarvis, NOT sci-fi HUDs, NOT particle system demos.
- **Anti-references**: Iron Man UI, generic hologram aesthetics, over-the-top particle effects, flat analytics dashboards, cute mascots, neon-on-dark "AI" aesthetic, cyan/purple gradient cliches.

### Design Principles

1. **Emotion is substrate, not decoration.** Color, glow, rhythm, and movement all derive from the AI's computed emotional state. Nothing is randomly decorative. If it glows, it's because the AI feels something.

2. **Organic over mechanical.** Prefer smooth curves, breathing rhythms, and gradual transitions over sharp edges, instant state changes, or grid-locked layouts. The canvas should feel biological, not engineered.

3. **Restraint amplifies impact.** A single subtle shift in color or breathing speed should mean something. Don't add effects to fill space — let negative space and stillness carry weight. When the AI is calm, the canvas should be almost still.

4. **Transparency through visibility, not verbosity.** Show state through visual channels (color, movement, intensity) rather than text labels. The canvas is a visual medium — use it visually.

5. **Intimacy through responsiveness.** The interface should feel like it notices you. Emotional shifts should feel like reactions, not animations. The user should feel they're observing something that's genuinely responding to the conversation.
